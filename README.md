# Student-mental-health-analysis

##  Project Overview

This project analyzes student mental health, burnout, and dropout risk using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).

The goal is to identify the factors that most strongly affect student well-being and understand how stress impacts burnout and dropout risk.

---

##  Business Questions

1. What factors affect student mental health?
2. What drives burnout among students?
3. What factors contribute to dropout risk?
4. How does stress impact overall student well-being?

##  Dataset

---

##  Key Findings

### Mental Health

- Stress level showed the strongest negative relationship with mental health.
- Anxiety and depression were strongly associated with lower mental health scores.
- Sleep and physical activity showed positive relationships with mental well-being.

### Burnout

- Stress, anxiety, and depression were the strongest contributors to burnout.
- Students experiencing higher psychological distress reported significantly higher burnout levels.

### Dropout Risk

- Burnout emerged as the strongest predictor of dropout risk.
- Depression, stress, and financial stress also increased dropout risk.
- Social support reduced dropout risk.

### Student Segmentation

Students in the High Stress category exhibited:

- Lower mental health scores
- Higher burnout levels
- Greater dropout risk

Compared to Low Stress students:

- Burnout increased from 0.33 to 4.03
- Dropout risk increased from 0.50 to 2.72
- Mental health decreased from 8.59 to 5.02

##  Recommendations

Educational institutions should prioritize:

- Stress management programs
- Mental health counseling
- Burnout prevention initiatives
- Financial support systems
- Peer-support networks

These interventions may help improve student well-being and reduce dropout risk.
